About the Founder

Kevin Wagner - Founder, Discrete Development

Systems Architect across meediums. Builder of modular, audit resistent workflows.

I didn’t start thinking this way when I moved into software.

Software is just the newest medium.

My perspective was shaped 30 years ago in broadcast television — designing facilities, installing systems, and serving as an implementation engineer where failure wasn’t theoretical. In Burbank, when someone turned on their television, programming appeared every time. The product of countless teams, rooms, departments, and systems was a continuous, uninterrupted broadcast signal. It never went offline. In all those years, it never failed. never went offline. It wsa not allowed to happen. That kind of pressure is almost unknown today. Back then, it was omni‑present.

Those same principles guide my work now.

I build systems that behave predictably in the real world — not just on paper.

I design flows that respect the user’s time, the operator’s sanity, and the truth of how data actually behaves.

And I architect solutions that stay calm under load, because I learned early that complexity is the enemy of reliability.

I don’t build around vendors or frameworks.

I build around the objective function — free of artificial constraints and platform loyalty.

But if your world runs inside the Microsoft monsterverses, I can work there too and still deliver the best, cleanest version of what you’re trying to achieve.

Discrete Development exists to bring that philosophy into modern operations:

  • ERP‑lite architectures
  • map‑based workflows
  • modular automations that are clear, resilient, and grounded in operational truth.

I don’t chase attention. I don’t chase hype.

I build systems that work — across mediums, across decades, across industries.
